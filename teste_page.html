About Me

Hi, I am Felippe, a 29 years old oceanographer from São Paulo, Brazil. When I was 19, I moved to Rio Grande, far south of Brazil, to study my main course and passion, Oceanography, at the Federal University of Rio Grande - FURG. In this course, we have subjects from the Chemistry, Biology, Geology and Physics Field. I chose Physical Oceanography to continue my path, which was the start of my journey into programming and technology.

During my undergrad period, I had the opportuniy to have a Scholarship sponsored by the National Council for Scientific and Technological Development (CNPq - Brazil) to study in Adelaide, Australia, for 15 months. More than an incredible academic moment, it was also a amazing personal and cultural experience in my life. In my period at the University of South Australia - UniSA, I studied subjects related with Environmental Sciences along two semesters. At the end of my exchange period, I was awarded with a Summer Scholarship from the Australian Research Council - Centre of Excellence for Climate System Science, when I then moved to Sydney to be a intern during 6 weeks at the Climate Change Reserch Centre (CCRC), in the University of New South Wales (UNSW).

Back in Brazil, I finished my degree and went back to São Paulo to work as a Data Analyst in a Digital Marketing Consultancy Agency. It was a challenge to be in such a different working environment, outside the academy. However, it was also a great opportunity for my professional development. Such job contributed to increasing my tech skills: I learned new technologies, key concepts of Business, company’s routine and, how to work in a data team.

Nowadays, in addition to my work, I spend my time studying and learning both technologies and oceanography/environmental sciences... Before the covid-19 and pandemic, I used to run, practice archery and was starting to climb (indoor), but since March is just books, Netflix, Amazon Prime and YouTube...
